# Competitor Research Skill
## Competitors to research
Primary competitors (outdoor focus — prioritise these):
- Gardeners Dream (gardenersdream.co.uk)
- You Garden (yougarden.com)
- Farmer Gracy (farmergracy.co.uk)
Secondary competitors (already researched — reference only):
- Patch Plants (patchplants.com)
- Lazy Flora (lazyflora.com)
- The Stem (thestem.co.uk)
## For each competitor
### Products
- Hero or best-selling outdoor product
- Fruit, veg, and edible plant range if available
- Price point and subscription offer if any
- Variants available
- Star rating and total review count
### Customer reviews — minimum 50 per brand
Positive themes ranked by mention count:
- What do customers love in their own words
- Which benefits get mentioned without being prompted
- Any recurring delight moments around delivery, packaging, outdoor plant quality
Negative themes ranked by mention count:
- Top complaints with mention counts
- What problem has no competitor solved
- What do customers wish was different
Flag immediately: any complaint that Carbeth Plants already solves
### Ad creative — Meta Ad Library
Research 10-20 active ads per brand.
Most repeating themes:
- What problem does the ad open with
- What benefit does it close on
- What lifestyle identity does it signal (garden, outdoor living, growing your own)
- Emotional tone: fear / aspiration / social proof / education / humour
Most repeating messages:
- Top 5 hook formulas — paraphrase, do not copy
- Most frequent benefit claims across all brands
- Buzzwords saturating the outdoor plant category that now feel invisible
Most repeating formats:
- List 4-6 dominant formats with description
- Note which brands use each format most
## Final output
- One summary table with one row per competitor
- Category-wide top 3 dominant themes
- Category-wide top 3 dominant formats
- Highest-volume negative review themes across all competitors
- Whitespace: what no competitor is saying that Carbeth customers want
- Recommended angle for Carbeth based on the gap found
